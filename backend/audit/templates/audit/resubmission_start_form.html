Resubmitting an Audit

Resubmitting an audit should not be undertaken lightly. A resubmission replaces the previously accepted audit and results in a new Federal Audit Clearinghouse (FAC) acceptance date. Because of this impact, resubmissions must be completed in coordination between the auditee and certifying officials. A resubmission can never change the UEI or the audit year from the original submission.

Before proceeding, review the guidance below to determine whether resubmission is appropriate. A resubmission is generally appropriate when material changes are required, including but not limited to:

  • Material change to audit findings
  • Addition or revision of corrective action plans
  • Inclusion of missing finding elements (such as condition, criteria, cause, or effect)
  • Correction to questioned costs or compliance determination
  • Error in major program determination or low-risk auditee status
  • Change to federal award amounts in the Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards (SEFA)
  • Correction to Assistance Listing Number
  • Supplementing an incomplete Single Audit package (for example, missing financial statements or required reports)
  • Addition or correction to the required auditor report elements
  • Revision to the auditor's report

Do not resubmit an audit due to non-material changes, including:

  • Minor typographical or formatting errors
  • Minor numerical rounding differences without material impact
  • Non-material errors that do not change audit opinions, findings, or compliance determinations
  • Updates that do not change audit opinions, findings, or compliance determinations
  • Issues documented for resolution in a future audit
  • SF-SAC-only corrections where the audit report itself is unchanged
  • Updates to contact information or other non-substantive metadata
  • Clarifications to narrative text that do not alter meaning

If your update falls into one of these categories, resubmission is not appropriate.

Resubmission must be completed in coordination with certifying officials, and resubmission will change the acceptance date for your audit.
